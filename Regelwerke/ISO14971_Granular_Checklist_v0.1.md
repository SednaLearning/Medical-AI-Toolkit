# ISO 14971:2019 Granular Checklist

---
## RM-001: Risk Management Process
**Reference:**
ISO 14971:2019 – Clause 4 (General requirements for risk management)
**Topic:**
Establishment and maintenance of the risk management process
**Requirement summary:**
The manufacturer must define, document, and maintain a structured risk management process tailored to the medical device's risk profile and integrated into the QMS.

---
### RM-001.1: Process Documentation
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- A **Risk Management Procedure (SOP)** exists and is approved by management.
- The SOP defines the **scope** (e.g., applicable product types, lifecycle phases).
- The SOP includes **roles and responsibilities** (e.g., Risk Management Officer, Design Team, QA).
- The SOP describes **interfaces** to other QMS processes (e.g., design control, CAPA, PMS).
**Expected evidence:**
- Approved Risk Management SOP
- Organizational chart with risk management roles
- QMS process map showing integration
**Common findings:**
- SOP is outdated or not aligned with ISO 14971:2019.
- Roles for risk management are not clearly defined.
- No evidence of management approval.

---
### RM-001.2: Lifecycle Coverage
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- The process covers **all lifecycle phases** (design, manufacturing, distribution, post-market).
- **Post-market surveillance (PMS)** is explicitly included.
- **Feedback loops** to design and production are defined.
**Expected evidence:**
- Risk Management Plan (RMP) for each product
- PMS Plan referencing risk management
- Design History File (DHF) with risk management links
**Common findings:**
- Post-market phase is missing or vague.
- No linkage between PMS and risk management.

---
### RM-001.3: Process Compliance
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- The process is **audited internally** at regular intervals.
- **Training records** for risk management personnel exist.
- **Non-conformities** from audits are addressed.
**Expected evidence:**
- Internal audit reports
- Training matrices for risk management
- CAPA records for process improvements
**Common findings:**
- No internal audits of the risk management process.
- Training gaps for personnel involved in risk management.

---
## RM-002: Risk Analysis
**Reference:**
ISO 14971:2019 – Clause 5
**Topic:**
Systematic identification and estimation of risks
**Requirement summary:**
The manufacturer must identify hazards, hazardous situations, and estimate risks using a systematic approach.

---
### RM-002.1: Hazard Identification
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **All potential hazards** (e.g., electrical, mechanical, biological, software) are identified.
- **Foreseeable misuse** (e.g., off-label use, user errors) is considered.
- **Hazardous situations** (e.g., environmental conditions, user interactions) are documented.
**Expected evidence:**
- Hazard Identification Report
- FMEA/HAZOP/ Fault Tree Analysis (FTA) records
- Use cases and misuse scenarios
**Common findings:**
- Hazards are only identified for normal use, not misuse.
- No systematic method (e.g., FMEA) is applied.

---
### RM-002.2: Risk Estimation
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Severity** and **probability** scales are defined (e.g., 1-5 or 1-10).
- Risk estimation is **quantitative or qualitative** (e.g., risk matrix).
- **Initial risk levels** are assigned to each hazard.
**Expected evidence:**
- Risk Analysis Report with severity/probability tables
- Risk matrix with defined thresholds
**Common findings:**
- Severity/probability scales are not justified.
- Risk estimation is inconsistent across hazards.

---
### RM-002.3: Documentation
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- Risk analysis is **documented in a traceable manner** (e.g., unique identifiers for hazards).
- **Design inputs** (e.g., intended use, user profile) are referenced.
- **Updates** to risk analysis are tracked (e.g., after design changes).
**Expected evidence:**
- Risk Analysis Report (version-controlled)
- Traceability matrix linking hazards to design inputs
- Change logs for risk analysis updates
**Common findings:**
- No traceability between hazards and design inputs.
- Risk analysis is not updated after design changes.

## RM-003: Risk Evaluation
**Reference:**
ISO 14971:2019 – Clause 6
**Topic:**
Evaluation of risk acceptability
**Requirement summary:**
The manufacturer must evaluate risks against predefined acceptability criteria and document decisions.

---
### RM-003.1: Risk Acceptability Criteria
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Risk acceptability criteria** are defined (e.g., ALARP principle, risk matrix thresholds).
- Criteria are **justified** (e.g., based on standards, clinical data).
- **Risk levels** (e.g., low, medium, high) are clearly defined.
**Expected evidence:**
- Risk Management Plan with acceptability criteria
- Justification document for criteria
**Common findings:**
- No defined risk acceptability criteria.
- Criteria are not aligned with industry standards.

---
### RM-003.2: Risk Comparison
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Each risk** is compared against the acceptability criteria.
- **Unacceptable risks** are flagged for reduction.
- **Acceptable risks** are justified (e.g., clinical benefit outweighs risk).
**Expected evidence:**
- Risk Evaluation Report
- Risk matrix with acceptability thresholds
**Common findings:**
- Risks are not systematically compared to criteria.
- No justification for acceptable risks.

---
### RM-003.3: Documentation
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Decisions** on risk acceptability are documented.
- **Rationale** for accepting risks is provided.
- **Stakeholder input** (e.g., clinical experts) is considered.
**Expected evidence:**
- Minutes of risk review meetings
- Signed-off Risk Evaluation Report
**Common findings:**
- No documentation of risk acceptability decisions.
- Missing stakeholder input.

---
## RM-004: Risk Control
**Reference:**
ISO 14971:2019 – Clause 7
**Topic:**
Implementation and verification of risk control measures
**Requirement summary:**
The manufacturer must implement controls to reduce risks to acceptable levels and verify their effectiveness.

---
### RM-004.1: Risk Control Measures
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Risk control measures** are identified for each unacceptable risk (e.g., design changes, warnings, training).
- Measures are **prioritized** (e.g., based on risk level).
- **Residual risks** are estimated after applying controls.
**Expected evidence:**
- Risk Control Plan
- Design changes linked to risk reduction
- Warning labels and IFU updates
**Common findings:**
- Risk controls are not linked to specific hazards.
- No prioritization of risk control measures.

---
### RM-004.2: Verification of Effectiveness
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Effectiveness** of risk controls is verified (e.g., testing, validation).
- **New hazards** introduced by controls are identified and assessed.
- **Verification results** are documented.
**Expected evidence:**
- Test reports (e.g., usability testing, biocompatibility)
- Updated FMEA/HAZOP after controls
- Verification protocols and reports
**Common findings:**
- No verification of risk control effectiveness.
- New hazards from controls are not assessed.

---
### RM-004.3: Documentation
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Risk control measures** are documented in the Risk Management File.
- **Traceability** between hazards, controls, and verification exists.
- **Updates** to risk assessments are tracked.
**Expected evidence:**
- Risk Management File (RMF)
- Traceability matrix
**Common findings:**
- No traceability between hazards and controls.
- Risk assessments are not updated after controls.

---
## RM-005: Evaluation of Overall Residual Risk
**Reference:**
ISO 14971:2019 – Clause 8
**Topic:**
Assessment of cumulative residual risk
**Requirement summary:**
The manufacturer must evaluate the overall residual risk, including cumulative effects, and ensure it is acceptable.

---
### RM-005.1: Overall Risk Assessment
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **All residual risks** (after controls) are aggregated.
- **Cumulative effects** of multiple risks are considered.
- **Overall residual risk** is compared to acceptability criteria.
**Expected evidence:**
- Overall Residual Risk Assessment Report
- Risk matrix with cumulative risk evaluation
**Common findings:**
- Cumulative risks are not assessed.
- No documentation of overall residual risk.

---
### RM-005.2: Risk Communication
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Residual risks** are communicated to users (e.g., via labeling, IFU).
- **Warnings and precautions** are clear and actionable.
- **User understanding** of risks is verified (e.g., usability testing).
**Expected evidence:**
- Labeling (e.g., warnings, precautions)
- Usability testing reports
**Common findings:**
- Residual risks are not communicated to users.
- Warnings are unclear or buried in documentation.

---
## RM-006: Risk Management Review
**Reference:**
ISO 14971:2019 – Clause 9
**Topic:**
Periodic review of the risk management process
**Requirement summary:**
The manufacturer must review the risk management process and its outputs at planned intervals and after significant changes.

---
### RM-006.1: Review Planning
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Review intervals** are defined (e.g., annual, after major changes).
- **Review inputs** include new information (e.g., complaints, field data, literature).
- **Review outputs** are documented (e.g., updated risk assessments).
**Expected evidence:**
- Risk Management Review Plan
- Meeting minutes (e.g., design reviews, PMS reviews)
**Common findings:**
- Reviews are not conducted at required intervals.
- New information is not incorporated into reviews.

---
### RM-006.2: Management Involvement
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **Management** is involved in risk management reviews.
- **Decisions** from reviews are implemented (e.g., CAPA initiation).
- **Escalation paths** for unresolved risks are defined.
**Expected evidence:**
- Management review records
- CAPA records linked to review findings
**Common findings:**
- No management involvement in reviews.
- No follow-up on review decisions.

---
## RM-007: Production and Post-Production Information
**Reference:**
ISO 14971:2019 – Clause 10
**Topic:**
Monitoring of production and post-market data
**Requirement summary:**
The manufacturer must collect and analyze production and post-production information to identify new hazards or risks.

---
### RM-007.1: Post-Market Surveillance (PMS)
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- A **PMS Plan** exists and is aligned with risk management.
- **Post-market data** (e.g., complaints, vigilance reports) is systematically collected.
- **Trends** in production data (e.g., non-conformities) are monitored.
**Expected evidence:**
- Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) Plan
- Complaint handling records
- Vigilance reports (e.g., MDR/IVDR reports)
**Common findings:**
- No systematic collection of post-market data.
- PMS is not linked to risk management.

---
### RM-007.2: Feedback Loop
**Assessment criteria:**
Check whether:
- **New hazards/risks** from PMS are assessed and addressed.
- **PMS findings** are fed back into the risk management process.
- **Corrective actions** (e.g., CAPA) are initiated for new risks.
**Expected evidence:**
- CAPA records linked to PMS findings
- Updated risk assessments based on PMS data
**Common findings:**
- No feedback loop from PMS to risk management.
- CAPAs are not initiated for new risks.
