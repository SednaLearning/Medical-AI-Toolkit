4. # **Executive Summary** {#executive-summary}

This Risk Management Report documents the results of the risk management activities performed for the OMNIA System, an investigational medical device intended for use within a controlled clinical investigation in the European Union under the Medical Devices Regulations (EU) 2017/745.

Risk management activities were conducted in accordance with ISO 14971 \[1\], as applied to investigational use. All reasonably foreseeable hazards associated with the investigational use of the OMNIA System were systematically identified, analyzed, and evaluated following the procedure Product Risk Management. Risk control measures were implemented following the hierarchy of inherent safety by design, protective measures, and information for safety.

In addition to product-specific controls, the structured clinical investigation environment constitutes an essential part of the risk mitigation strategy. Mandatory participant and investigator training, predefined monitoring procedures, and established escalation pathways, as defined in the Clinical Investigation Plan, Investigator’s Brochure, and study-specific Instructions for Use, are considered formal risk control measures.

Following implementation of all risk control measures, no residual risks remain in the non-acceptable region. Residual risks classified as AFAP (As Far As Possible) are considered acceptable exclusively within the defined investigational context.

While no direct therapeutic benefit is anticipated for study participants, the anticipated scientific and technical knowledge gained from the feasibility evaluation of the OMNIA System is considered to justify the residual risks under the controlled study conditions.

Based on the completed risk management activities and benefit–risk assessment, the overall residual risk of the OMNIA System is considered acceptable for investigational use within the proposed clinical investigation.

5. # **Purpose** {#purpose}

This Risk Management Report identifies the risk-management activities for the OMNIA APS system throughout the design, manufacturing, and post-production lifecycle. It is prepared to support an application for Investigational Testing under the Medical Devices Regulation.  
This Risk Management Report documents the performance of the final evaluation and approval of the implemented risk management for the OMNIA APS System.  
The final evaluation should ensure

* that the risk management plan has been implemented appropriately,  
* that undesirable effects are acceptable from a pre-clinical point of view,  
* that the overall residual risk has been reduced as low as reasonably practicable and that it is acceptable in the clinical investigation context,  
* that the benefit is given in relation to the overall residual risk,

9. # **Risk benefit analysis** {#risk-benefit-analysis}

The risk–benefit analysis was performed in accordance with ISO 14971 and MDR Annex I, taking into account the investigational nature of the device and the controlled clinical investigation environment.  
Assessment of medical benefit and justification of the acceptance of the overall residual risk  
There are no remaining risks in the not acceptable area (NACC).  
There are some risks that by their nature will always be evaluated as having a high severity of harm. Such risks are widely understood as being inherent to the nature of such a product. In these cases, all reasonable efforts have been made to minimize the overall risk picture by ensuring the use of best practices and good design to minimize the probability of the risk occurring.  
Residual risks categorized as AFAP are considered acceptable only because the investigational device is used under continuous supervision by trained healthcare professionals, with predefined escalation and intervention procedures as described in the Study Protocol \[6\]. All residual risks will be appropriately communicated to investigators and study participants via the study-specific Instructions for Use, Investigator’s Brochure, and informed consent materials.

10. # **Summary of Evaluation Results** {#summary-of-evaluation-results}

The risk management activities for the OMNIA System were performed in accordance with the approved Risk Management Plan and ISO 14971\. All reasonably foreseeable hazards associated with the investigational use of the device were identified, analyzed, evaluated, and controlled.

All defined risk control measures have been implemented and verified, and no new risks were introduced as a result of these measures. Following implementation of risk controls, no risks remain in the non-acceptable (NACC) area.

Residual risks classified as AFAP are considered acceptable in the context of the proposed clinical investigation, taking into account the controlled study environment, continuous supervision by trained healthcare professionals, and predefined monitoring and escalation procedures.

Based on the overall residual risk evaluation and risk–benefit analysis, the OMNIA System is considered to have an acceptable overall residual risk for use within the proposed clinical investigation.
